MiDOC - Potential Sources of Error Calculations

  • Sources of error
    • Movement of the eye / patient / surgeon
    • Oscillations induced by swinging of the bag on the IV pole
    • Kinks in-line
    • Leveling
      • Height of P3 and P4 (due to an unlevel surface)
    • Oscillations/fluctuations induced when raising the IV bag
    • Vibrations in the room / environment
      • People walking
      • Surrounding equipment (Heating / AC, Cooling fans in nearby electronics)
    • Inline air bubbles
    • Leaks
    • Sensor Calibration
    • Temperature and Pressure in the room
    • In line flow vectoring
      • Rotation/Orientation of the needle (how straight it is inserted and which way the opening is facing)
      • Location of the needle (how centered it is when inserted)
    • In line Blockages / Deposits
  • Accuracy required for measurement of Eyelasticity™

Goal

Determine how consistently different pressure sensors of the same type scale in output when exposed to varying magnitudes of pressure

Setup

  1. Elevated IV Bag -> P3 -> P4 -> Stop Cock
  2. Fill up the IV bag to 45 mmHg
  3. Open the stop cock and let the water drain until the pressure drops by 8 mmHg
  4. Stop the stop cock
  5. Take 5 s of data with the MiDOC
  6. Append the data to a file titled with the index of the downstream sensor
  7. repeat steps (2-6) 4 times
  8. Once the fluid runs out switch out the downstream pressure sensor with a new one and create a new pressure sensor file

Load the files and analyze.py to analyze the standard deviation of the pressure differential between the sensors
