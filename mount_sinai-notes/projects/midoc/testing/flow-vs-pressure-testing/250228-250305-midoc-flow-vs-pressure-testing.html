Objective

Note

This test is to calibrate the Pressure Flow (PF) relationship.
It is not indicative of the actual setup that the MiDOC will be used in!
That test will be done later

Develop a relationship between PDiff (P4-P3) and the flow reported by the flow sensor

Note that we do not actually care what the real flow from the iv bag is as of now because we just want the pressure sensor data to match perfectly with the flow sensor data.

Testing setup

  1. Hook up the MiDOC in between an IV bag and a 3 ml syringe hooked up to the WPI Pressure infusion pump.
  2. Wait for the pressure to equalize, meaning no flow is going through the sensors.
  3. Run calibrate_flow_test.py on the WPI which runs a 10 minute test that cycles through a variety of flow rates.

Testing Plans

Does the height of the IV bag have an effect on the PF Correlation?

Reality

it has no effect

Reality

Are the pressure sensors out of calibration straight out of the box?

Hypothesis

The scaling discrepancy is caused by out of calibration sensors.

Procedure

Offset

  1. plug in some pressure sensors
  2. check how different they are from each other in air at the same height

Scale

  1. Tare 2 pressure sensors connected to a column of water at a low height
  2. Change the height of the column of water
  3. check to see how off they are

Reality

Offset

They can be off by at least 2 mmHg (potentially more) straight out of the box in offset.

Scale

by inspection, it likely isn't scaled, just offset. This will be verified quantitatively later

Conclusions

  • The height of the IV bag has negligible effect on the Delta P - Flow Correlation
  • The pressure sensors can be off by at least 2 mmHg straight out of the box in offset which causes varying slopes on the Delta P - Flow Correaltion
    • the pressure sensors need to be reliably tared
