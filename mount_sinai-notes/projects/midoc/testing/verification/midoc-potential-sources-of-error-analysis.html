Objective

To identify sources of error for the MiDOC device, their magnitudes, and the methods to mitigate them.

All Potential Sources of Error

  • Movement of the eye / patient / surgeon
  • Oscillations of the IV Bag
  • Blockages & Kinks in the Line
  • Leveling
  • Vibrations in the room / Enviroment
    • People walking
    • Surrounding equipment (Heating / AC, Cooling fans in nearby electronics)
  • Air In Line
  • Leaks
  • Raising Bag Live
  • Sensor Calibration
  • Temperature
  • In Line Flow Vectoring
    • Rotation/Orientation of the needle (how straight it is inserted and which way the opening is facing)
    • Location of the needle (how centered it is when inserted)
  • IOP Calculation from P4

Sources of Error Calculations

Movement of the Eye, Patient, & Surgeon

Effect: Extremely High
Mitigation: Everyone needs to stay still during data collection.

When raising and lowering the IV bag normally, it is unlikely to swing to a full 10 degrees. the amplitude and decay time of oscillations when raising and lowering the IV bag is tested in 250318 - MiDOC Bag Oscillation Testing which finds a maximum decay time of 12 seconds.

Mitigation: Wait 15 seconds after raising or lowering the IV bag prior to starting data collection. (Also pass visual inspection that the IV bag is not swinging)

Blockages / Kinks in the Line

Effect: High. This would be a dominate effect on the testing.
Mitigation: Ensure no blockages / kinks in the line prior to testing.

Air In Line

Effect: Air bubbles trapped in the system introduce compliance, reducing the measured pressure difference.
Mitigation: Lines must be purged entirely prior to use of the MiDOC. Failure to do so will result in wildly varying results. Further, when priming the Infusion Set, we must open the air relief valve and squeeze the vial until largely full to ensure no air bubbles get sucked down the iv tube from the falling droplets.

This is demonstrated in 250310 - MiDOC Flow vs Pressure Relationship > Batch 3 where the flow calculations are off by 10 to 15 between batches when there is air in line.

Leaks

Effect: Any leak between the pressure sensors or after the pressure sensors in line will overshadow any calculation
Mitigation: Ensure the calculated flow is near zero when the stop cock is closed prior to the test. Use a proper silicon injection site instead of putting a needle through the side of an IV tube.

Was likely the major source of error in 250307 - MiDOC Flow vs Pressure Relationship > Batch 1 and 2 with a maximum measured difference in flow of around 5 for the same pressure drop

Raising Bag Live

Effect: Raising the IV Bag while being connected to the pressure sensors creates a large amount of oscillations and data noise from that inhibits proper measurement.

Mitigation: Using a Stop Cock in between the IV Bag and Pressure Sensor makes it possible to stop the flow before raising the bag. This prevents the vibrations caused by the shaking of the IV bag from transmitting to the pressure sensors.

Sensor Calibration

Effect: The scaling and accuracy of the pressure sensors is documented in the testing: 250318 - MiDOC Pressure Sensor Consistency Testing

Goal

Determine how consistently different pressure sensors of the same type scale in output when exposed to varying magnitudes of pressusre

Setup

  1. Elevated IV Bag -> P3 -> P4 -> Stop Cock
  2. Fill up the IV bag to 45 mmHg
  3. Open the stop cock and let the water drain until the pressure drops by 8 mmHg
  4. Stop the stop cock
  5. Take 5 s of data with the MiDOC
  6. Append the data to a file titled with the index of the downstream sensor
  7. repeat steps (2-6) 4 times
  8. Once the fluid runs out switch out the downstream pressure sensor with a new one and create a new pressure sensor file

Load the files and analyze.py to analyze the standard deviation of the pressure differential between the sensors

Results

Overall Average Standard Deviation: 0.03 mm Hg

Mitigation: No mitigation is needed on this front as the average standard deviation of these sensors is 0.03 mm Hg.

From 70mmHg, this offset would be a 0.14 mm Hg difference. Fortunately, this should cancel out in the flow calculation math and only the pressure drop across the needle would have a 0.2% offset which, with a pressure drop of 5.0mmHg, would then yield a pressure offset of 0.01mmHg.

Mitigation: No mitigation is required for the temperature of these sensors as we are largely looking at the offset and therefore the effect of the temperature is minimal.
